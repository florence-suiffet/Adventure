Itinerary

2 weeks in Mexico

Mexico is a beautiful country. It is also a gigantic country (4 times the size of France) where a multitude of trips are possible and that is why you are right to plan your travel itinerary in advance.

Here is our best itinerary for a 2 weeks trip to Mexico, you will discover the capital Mexico City and its lively districts, then the magnificent coloured region and finally Puerto Escondido and its surroundings to enjoy the sun and the beaches. Nice sunny reading.

Day 1 - 5

Discovery of Mexico City

Day 1: Museum of Anthropology and Zocalo (historical centre). Visit the Museum of Anthropology and the historical centre.

Day 2: Teotihuacan and the Basilica of Guadalupe. This is the largest archaeological site in Mexico and without doubt one of the most spectacular and a must-see.

Day 3: Xochimilco. Visit Coyoacán, arguably the most charming neighbourhood in the capital.

Day 4: Discover Mexico City's trendiest neighbourhoods: La Roma and La Condesa.

Day 5: Walk around and visit some museums like the Fine Arts Museum, Frida Kahlo, Templo Mayor...

Day 6 - 9

Discovery of Puerto Escondido

Day 6: Playa Carrizalillo

Day 7: La Punta y la Playa Zicatela. Attend the launching of baby turtles, take a boat ride to see whales and dolphins

Day 8: Surf and discover Playa Manzanillo

Day 9: Swim in the luminescent plankton

Some good addresses:

Ala Burger: gigantic pizza on the grill (to share) for 120 pesos (6€) / burger 100 pesos (4€). The portions are generous and the atmosphere very nice.

Lychee: 130 pesos a plate (6-7€), it's a Thai restaurant with its feet in the sand, a small, subdued atmosphere and delicious dishes.

AMOKI Restaurante: 100 pesos for a burrito (4€) but what a burrito! They are HUGE and delicious with fish.

Day 10 - 14

Discovery of Oaxaca

Day 10: Visit El Zocalo, the Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzman & lunch in one of the markets. Afternoon: printmaking workshop & city walk.

Day 11: Participate in an indigenous purification ceremony: Temazcal. Lunch in one of the fancy restaurants. Afternoon: Mezcal tasting in a mezcalaria. Night in Oaxaca

Day 12: Visit Hierve el Agua (and possibly the ruins of Mitla).

Day 13: Visit the market. Take a weaving or dyeing class in Teotilan del Valle.

Day 14: Visit a mezcal factory. Visit the Tlacolula market, the largest tree in the world in Santa Maria del Tule.
